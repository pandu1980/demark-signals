## What Does Palantir Actually Do?

Palantir has three main products:

**Gotham** — Built for government and defense. It helps agencies connect data from different sources, spot patterns, and make decisions faster. Think counter-terrorism, military logistics, and intelligence work.

**Foundry** — The commercial version. It does the same thing for businesses — connecting siloed data and turning it into actionable insights. Used by healthcare companies, manufacturers, and financial institutions.

**AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform)** — This is the growth engine. AIP lets customers deploy large language models on their own private data. Unlike ChatGPT, everything stays secure within the organization's infrastructure. This is what's driving the explosive commercial growth.

## Recent Wins

The momentum is real:

- **DISA Authorization (Feb 2026):** Palantir secured IL5/IL6 accreditation for PFCS Forward, enabling on-premises and edge deployments for defense. This opens doors to more sensitive government workloads.

- **Cognizant Partnership:** Palantir Foundry and AIP are being embedded into Cognizant's TriZetto healthcare business — a major win in a highly regulated industry.

- **Airbus Extension:** Multi-year Skywise data platform deal extended, with 50,000+ daily users. Aviation is becoming a stronghold.

- **Rackspace Collaboration:** New enterprise AI deployment partnership targeting regulated industries.

## The Bull Case 🐂

### 1. AIP Is a Game-Changer
Palantir isn't just adding AI features — they've built a platform that lets enterprises safely use generative AI without exposing sensitive data. The 137% U.S. commercial growth proves customers are buying in.

### 2. Government Business Is Sticky
Once a government agency integrates Palantir, switching costs are enormous. The DISA authorization expands their addressable market in defense. The U.S. defense budget isn't shrinking anytime soon.

### 3. Commercial Growth Is Accelerating
For years, critics said Palantir couldn't crack the commercial market. That narrative is dead. 137% growth and 139% net dollar retention tell the story.

### 4. Profitability Is Real
$608M net income in Q4 alone. This isn't a cash-burning startup — it's a profitable company with massive free cash flow guidance ($3.9–$4.1B for 2026).

---

## The Bear Case 🐻

### 1. Valuation Is Stretched
At ~$131/share, Palantir trades at a premium. The stock is down ~26% YTD in 2026 despite the strong earnings — showing how much growth is already priced in.

### 2. Customer Concentration
A significant portion of revenue comes from a small number of large contracts. Losing one major customer would hurt.

### 3. Stock-Based Compensation
Palantir has historically been generous with stock-based compensation, which dilutes shareholders. This is improving but worth monitoring.

### 4. Insider Sales
Recent insider selling has raised eyebrows. Watch the Form 4 filings.

## The Bottom Line

Palantir is executing at an elite level. The numbers don't lie — 70% revenue growth, 137% U.S. commercial growth, and guidance that crushed expectations.

Yes, the valuation is rich. Yes, the stock is volatile. But when a company is growing this fast, is profitable, and has a clear AI moat, I'm willing to pay up.

**My take:** I'm bullish on Palantir. The recent pullback from highs creates an opportunity to build a position. This is one of the few pure-play AI infrastructure companies that's actually profitable and growing at scale. The AIP platform is a genuine competitive advantage, and the commercial momentum is undeniable. I'm a buyer on weakness.
